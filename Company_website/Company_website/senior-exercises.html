SENIOR CITIZENS EXERCISES

These exercises are specifically designed for senior citizens to improve mobility, balance, and overall well-being. Always consult with your healthcare provider before starting any new exercise program.

Chair Sit-to-Stand

Benefits: Improves leg strength and balance

Instructions:

  1. Sit in a sturdy chair with feet flat on the floor
  2. Lean slightly forward and stand up slowly
  3. Slowly sit back down with control
  4. Repeat 10 times, rest, then do another set if possible

Modifications: Use armrests for support if needed

Wall Push-Ups

Benefits: Strengthens arms, chest, and shoulders

Instructions:

  1. Stand facing a wall, arms length away
  2. Place hands on the wall at shoulder height
  3. Bend elbows to bring your body toward the wall
  4. Push back to starting position
  5. Repeat 10-15 times

Modifications: Adjust distance from wall for difficulty

Ankle Circles

Benefits: Improves ankle mobility and circulation

Instructions:

  1. Sit in a chair with feet flat on the floor
  2. Lift one foot slightly off the ground
  3. Rotate your ankle in a circular motion 10 times clockwise
  4. Rotate 10 times counterclockwise
  5. Repeat with the other foot

Modifications: Support leg with hands if needed

Seated Marching

Benefits: Improves hip flexibility and core strength

Instructions:

  1. Sit tall in a chair with feet flat on the floor
  2. Lift one knee up toward the ceiling, then lower it
  3. Alternate lifting each knee in a marching motion
  4. Continue for 30-60 seconds

Modifications: Hold onto chair for stability if needed

SAFETY GUIDELINES

Before You Begin

  • Consult with your healthcare provider before starting any new exercise program
  • Ensure you have a sturdy chair and clear space around you
  • Wear comfortable, non-slip shoes
  • Have water nearby to stay hydrated
  • Stop immediately if you experience pain, dizziness, or shortness of breath
