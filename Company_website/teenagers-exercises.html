TEENAGER EXERCISES

These exercises are specifically designed for teenagers to improve strength, flexibility, and overall fitness. Always use proper form and start with lighter weights if you're new to exercise.

Bodyweight Squats

Benefits: Builds leg strength and improves mobility

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart
  2. Keep your chest up and back straight
  3. Lower your body as if sitting in a chair
  4. Keep knees aligned with toes
  5. Return to standing position
  6. Repeat for 3 sets of 15 reps

Modifications: Hold onto a stable surface for balance if needed

Modified Push-Ups

Benefits: Strengthens chest, shoulders, and arms

Instructions:

  1. Start in a plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulders
  2. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels
  3. Lower your chest toward the floor
  4. Push back up to starting position
  5. Repeat for 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Modifications: Perform on knees for beginners

Forearm Plank

Benefits: Strengthens core, shoulders, and back

Instructions:

  1. Start in a forearm plank position with elbows under shoulders
  2. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels
  3. Engage your core and glutes
  4. Hold the position for 30 seconds
  5. Rest and repeat 3 times

Modifications: Perform on knees for beginners

Jumping Jacks

Benefits: Improves cardiovascular fitness and coordination

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet together and arms at your sides
  2. Jump while spreading legs and raising arms above head
  3. Jump again to return to starting position
  4. Perform 3 sets of 30 seconds each

Modifications: Step out instead of jumping for lower impact

SAFETY GUIDELINES

Important Safety Tips for Teenagers

  • Always warm up before exercising and cool down afterward
  • Use proper form to prevent injuries
  • Stay hydrated before, during, and after exercise
  • Listen to your body and rest if you feel pain (not just muscle fatigue)
  • Increase intensity gradually as you build strength
  • Consult with a healthcare provider before starting a new exercise program, especially if you have any health concerns
