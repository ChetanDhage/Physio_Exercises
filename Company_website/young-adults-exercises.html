YOUNG ADULTS EXERCISES

These exercises are designed for young adults to build strength, improve cardiovascular fitness, and enhance overall physical performance. Always warm up properly before starting your workout.

Burpees

Benefits: Full-body workout that builds strength and cardio endurance

Instructions:

  1. Start in a standing position
  2. Drop into a squat position with hands on the ground
  3. Kick feet back into a plank position
  4. Perform a push-up (optional)
  5. Return feet to squat position
  6. Jump up from squat position
  7. Repeat for 3 sets of 10-15 reps

Modifications: Skip the push-up or jump for a lower-intensity version

Walking Lunges

Benefits: Strengthens legs, improves balance and coordination

Instructions:

  1. Stand with feet hip-width apart
  2. Step forward with right leg into a lunge position
  3. Lower left knee toward the floor (without touching)
  4. Push through right heel to stand
  5. Step forward with left leg and repeat
  6. Perform 3 sets of 10 lunges per leg

Modifications: Hold onto a wall for balance or perform stationary lunges

Mountain Climbers

Benefits: Builds core strength and cardiovascular endurance

Instructions:

  1. Start in a high plank position with hands under shoulders
  2. Keep your core engaged and back flat
  3. Drive right knee toward chest
  4. Quickly switch legs, bringing left knee toward chest
  5. Continue alternating legs at a quick pace
  6. Perform 3 sets of 30 seconds each

Modifications: Slow down the pace or perform on an incline for less intensity

Russian Twists

Benefits: Strengthens obliques and core muscles

Instructions:

  1. Sit on the floor with knees bent
  2. Lean back slightly, keeping back straight
  3. Lift feet slightly off the ground (optional)
  4. Clasp hands together in front of chest
  5. Twist torso to the right, then to the left
  6. Perform 3 sets of 15 twists per side

Modifications: Keep feet on the ground or use a light weight for added resistance

SAFETY GUIDELINES

Important Safety Tips for Young Adults

  • Always perform a proper warm-up before starting your workout
  • Focus on form over speed or weight to prevent injuries
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workout
  • Allow adequate rest between workout sessions
  • Progress gradually by increasing intensity, duration, or resistance
  • Listen to your body and modify exercises as needed
  • Consult with a fitness professional if you're unsure about proper technique
